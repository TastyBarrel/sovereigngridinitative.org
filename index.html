The Vision
"A modern abundant power grid is the interstate highway system of the 21st century."

When Eisenhower built the interstate system the stated purpose was national defense. The second order effects were transformational Ś suburban growth, the trucking industry, economic integration across the country. Nobody could fully predict what abundant connected infrastructure would unlock. We are making the same bet for electricity.

  • Energy independenceNo more power prices spiking when a war breaks out in Iran or a regime changes in the Middle East
  • Water securityDesalination plants on US coastlines Ś power-intensive but transformative for water-scarce regions
  • Transportation transformationElectrification, high speed rail, and public transit powered by abundant domestic energy
  • Hydrogen as domestic fuelGenerated from water Ś a clean homegrown energy source that ends petroleum dependence
  • Manufacturing revivalNot just chips Ś broad industrial reshoring made viable by abundant cheap power
  • Defense readinessA grid that can survive conflict, cyberattack, and extreme weather Ś a true national security asset

The Problem
"The nation that controls baseload power controls the data centers. The nation that controls the data centers shapes AI. The nation that shapes AI shapes the global order."
  • AI is consuming chips and gigawatts at a pace the grid was never designed to handle
  • Chip manufacturing must reshore from Taiwan Ś but domestic fabs require baseload power that doesn't exist at the needed scale
  • Defense modernization depends on chip supply security we cannot currently guarantee
  • Renewables have papered over the problem with effective GW rather than real dispatchable baseload
  • Nuclear and hydro Ś the obvious answers Ś remain hamstrung by regulation and outdated sentiment
  • The grid's components themselves come from geopolitical competitors Ś a vulnerability hiding in plain sight
  • Nobody is speaking with one voice across all of these domains. Until now.
